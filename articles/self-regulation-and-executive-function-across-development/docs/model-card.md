# Model Card

## Name

Synthetic self-regulation and executive-function trajectory model.

## Intended use

Research education, article companion materials, methods teaching, longitudinal analysis practice, executive-function modeling, self-regulation modeling, and reproducible workflow development.

## Inputs

Synthetic variables representing baseline executive function, caregiving support, classroom structure, sleep quality, chronic stress, temperament reactivity, disability support need, school climate, regulation scaffolding, disability accommodation, transition predictability, acute stress, intervention exposure, regulatory support context, and regulation score.

## Outputs

Longitudinal synthetic panel, profile summaries, school/context summaries, dynamic regression outputs, trajectory plots, scenario comparisons, and validation summaries.

## Data notes

All generated data are synthetic and parameterized for transparency. The design is suitable for demonstration and extension.

## Limitations

The workflow uses simplified mechanisms, simulated parameters, no real participant data, no causal identification strategy, and no validated executive-function, self-regulation, stress, sleep, disability-support, or classroom-climate measurement scale.

## Extension paths

The structure can be extended with validated executive-function tasks, school records, observation data, sleep measures, intervention dosage, physiological stress measures, disability support plans, classroom context measures, mixed-method interviews, and stronger causal designs.
