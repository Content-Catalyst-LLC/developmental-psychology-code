# Methods

## Overview

This workflow creates a synthetic longitudinal panel for self-regulation and executive-function research. It demonstrates how regulation can be represented across repeated waves and nested school contexts.

## Design

The synthetic panel includes:

- child/adolescent baseline characteristics;
- repeated observation waves;
- school climate, regulation scaffolding, disability accommodation, and transition predictability;
- time-varying caregiving support, classroom structure, sleep quality, stress, and intervention exposure;
- disability support need and accommodation effects;
- regulatory support context;
- broad regulation outcome;
- regulation profiles by stress and support context.

## Modeling strategy

The repository uses synthetic data generation, dynamic regression, multilevel modeling, scenario comparison, SQL summaries, and compiled-language validation utilities.

## Interpretation

The models illustrate self-regulation, executive function, co-regulation, stress, sleep, classroom structure, intervention exposure, disability support, and environmental fit. They are scaffolds for reasoning, teaching, and methodological extension.
