# Model Card

## Name

Synthetic continuity-discontinuity developmental-change model.

## Intended use

Research education, reproducible-method scaffolding, continuity/discontinuity critique, threshold modeling, logistic transition modeling, and scenario demonstration.

## Not intended for

Developmental screening, maturity scoring, trauma prediction, clinical diagnosis, school placement, disability classification, student tracking, child ranking, eligibility decisions, surveillance, or any high-stakes decision-making.

## Inputs

Synthetic variables representing baseline functioning, growth rate, curvature, threshold timing, threshold sensitivity, support, chronic stress, institutional rupture, intervention exposure, context support, resource stability, transition readiness, and developmental outcome.

## Outputs

Synthetic developmental trajectories, threshold summaries, profile summaries, transition-readiness summaries, and scenario comparisons.

## Limitations

- No empirical validation.
- No causal evidence.
- No person-level predictive validity.
- No validated clinical, educational, or developmental instrument.
- Synthetic parameter values.
- Simplified context and threshold structure.
- Potential misuse if interpreted as real developmental analytics.

## Ethical position

The model treats continuity and discontinuity as conceptual and methodological objects, not as labels for real people. It should never be used to classify, rank, or predict children, adolescents, adults, cultures, disabled people, neurodivergent people, schools, families, or communities.
