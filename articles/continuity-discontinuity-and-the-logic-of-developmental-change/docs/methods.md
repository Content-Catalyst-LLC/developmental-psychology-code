# Methods

## Overview

This workflow models continuity and discontinuity as alternative but compatible shapes of developmental change. The synthetic data include repeated observations of individuals nested in contexts, with continuous growth, nonlinear curvature, threshold-sensitive transitions, logistic transition, time-varying support, chronic stress, intervention exposure, rupture exposure, resource stability, and transition readiness.

## Variables

- baseline functioning
- linear growth rate
- nonlinear curvature
- threshold timing
- threshold sensitivity
- logistic transition
- contextual support
- chronic stress
- institutional rupture
- intervention exposure
- school/context support
- resource stability
- transition readiness
- developmental outcome

## Conceptual interpretation

### Continuous growth

Continuous growth represents cumulative developmental change.

### Nonlinear growth

Nonlinear growth represents acceleration or deceleration without a categorical transition.

### Threshold transition

Threshold transition represents a discontinuous or stage-like shift after an individual reaches a transition point.

### Logistic transition

Logistic transition represents a smoother transition zone, where development accelerates around a threshold and then stabilizes.

### Institutional rupture

Institutional rupture represents discontinuity produced by context: school exclusion, displacement, care disruption, health crisis, violence, housing loss, or other system-level disruption.

### Intervention exposure

Intervention exposure represents supportive turning points: therapy, stable placement, accommodation, mentoring, income support, school reform, or public-health support.

## Synthetic-data caution

The data are not empirical findings. They are simulated for reproducible demonstration only.
