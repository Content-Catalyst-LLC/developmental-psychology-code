# Research Notes

## Article

**Continuity, Discontinuity, and the Logic of Developmental Change**

## Purpose of this code folder

This folder translates the article's developmental argument into a reproducible, multi-language research scaffold. It treats continuity and discontinuity as possible shapes of developmental change rather than rival slogans. It models gradual growth, nonlinear growth, threshold-sensitive transitions, logistic transitions, contextual support, chronic stress, resource stability, intervention-like supports, institutional rupture, and transition readiness.

## Core research commitments

1. **Continuity and discontinuity often operate together**  
   Development may be continuous in mechanism and discontinuous in appearance.

2. **Thresholds can be modeled without rigid stage theory**  
   Stage-like shifts can be represented as threshold or logistic transition effects without assuming universal age scripts.

3. **Context can create or buffer discontinuity**  
   Family support, school support, resource stability, stress, rupture, trauma, intervention, and institutional conditions alter developmental trajectories.

4. **Measurement scale matters**  
   Coarse measurement may exaggerate discontinuity; fine-grained measurement may reveal cumulative processes.

5. **Inequality changes developmental time**  
   Unequal care, schooling, disability accommodation, safety, housing, healthcare, and recognition shape whether pathways remain stable or become ruptured.

6. **Ethical caution is part of the method**  
   These scripts are synthetic demonstrations, not screening, ranking, placement, clinical, or high-stakes decision tools.

## What makes the folder research-grade

- synthetic longitudinal data with repeated waves;
- person-level, context-level, and time-varying variables;
- continuous growth, nonlinear growth, threshold shifts, logistic transitions, support, stress, resource stability, intervention exposure, rupture exposure, and transition readiness;
- dynamic modeling with lagged outcomes;
- threshold/logistic and transition-readiness interaction modeling;
- scenario analysis for support, stress reduction, intervention, rupture, resource stability, and combined protective conditions;
- SQL schema and analytical queries;
- Python and R workflows;
- compiled-language validation and summary utilities;
- explicit ethics and reproducibility notes;
- notes in every subdirectory.
