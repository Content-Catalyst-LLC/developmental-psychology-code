# Methods

## Overview

This workflow models wisdom, meaning, and development in later life as a longitudinal process. The synthetic data include older adults observed repeatedly across waves and nested within care/community contexts.

The model includes:

- baseline meaning;
- social connection;
- reflective integration;
- health burden;
- adaptive support;
- legacy orientation;
- dignity support;
- service access;
- community participation;
- wisdom index;
- meaning score;
- meaning profile.

## Conceptual interpretation

### Wisdom index

The wisdom index is a synthetic teaching construct combining reflective integration, social connection, legacy orientation, dignity support, and health burden. It should not be interpreted as a validated wisdom scale.

### Meaning score

The meaning score represents broad later-life meaning development. It is shaped by prior meaning state, social connection, reflection, health, support, legacy, dignity, services, and community participation.

### Care context

Care context represents household, neighborhood, institutional, community, or health-service environments that shape dignity, access, and participation.

### Health burden

Health burden is modeled as a developmental constraint, not as the whole meaning of later life.

## Synthetic-data caution

The data are not empirical findings. They are simulated for reproducible demonstration only.
