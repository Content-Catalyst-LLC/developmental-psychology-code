# Wisdom, Meaning, and Later-Life Development Framework

## Core constructs

| Construct | Developmental meaning |
|---|---|
| Social connection | Relationship quality, belonging, being known, emotional support |
| Reflective integration | Life review, narrative coherence, perspective-taking, interpretation |
| Health burden | Pain, illness, mobility limitation, cognitive strain, fatigue, care need |
| Adaptive support | Practical, emotional, technological, and environmental support |
| Legacy orientation | Generativity, transmission, memory, intergenerational value |
| Dignity support | Respect, voice, autonomy, privacy, cultural recognition, humane care |
| Service access | Health care, transportation, social programs, home support, prevention |
| Community participation | Belonging, civic role, ritual, shared activity, meaningful contribution |
| Wisdom index | Synthetic combination of reflection, connection, legacy, dignity, and burden |
| Meaning score | Synthetic outcome representing broad later-life meaning development |

## Interpretation

This workflow does not treat wisdom as guaranteed by age or meaning as private willpower. It models wisdom and meaning as developmental processes shaped by reflection, relation, health, support, dignity, and care environments.
