# Research Notes

## Article

**Wisdom, Meaning, and Development in Later Life**

## Purpose of this code folder

This folder translates the article's developmental argument into a reproducible, multi-language research scaffold. It treats later-life wisdom and meaning as developmental, relational, embodied, and institutionally shaped rather than as automatic products of age or private personality.

## Core research commitments

1. **Later life is developmentally active**  
   Older adults continue to interpret memory, revise values, adapt to constraint, sustain relationships, and seek meaning.

2. **Wisdom is not automatic with age**  
   Wisdom is modeled as reflective integration, social connection, legacy orientation, dignity support, and health burden together.

3. **Meaning is path-dependent and contextual**  
   Prior meaning organization shapes later meaning, but social connection, health, support, and care context can redirect trajectories.

4. **Health constraint is developmental**  
   Health burden is modeled as a condition shaping meaning, not as a total definition of older adulthood.

5. **Dignity and care systems matter**  
   Dignity support, service access, and community participation are represented as developmental conditions.

6. **Ethical caution is part of the method**  
   These scripts are synthetic demonstrations, not clinical, mental-health, elder-care, insurance, or policy prediction tools.

## What makes the folder research-grade

- synthetic panel data with repeated later-life waves;
- person-level, care-context-level, and time-varying variables;
- social connection, reflective integration, health burden, adaptive support, legacy orientation, dignity support, service access, community participation, wisdom, and meaning;
- dynamic later-life meaning modeling;
- wisdom and meaning trajectory summaries;
- scenario analysis for connection, reflection, adaptive support, health reduction, dignity, service access, community participation, and combined support;
- SQL schema and analytical queries;
- Python and R workflows;
- compiled-language validation and summary utilities;
- explicit ethics and reproducibility notes;
- notes in every subdirectory.
