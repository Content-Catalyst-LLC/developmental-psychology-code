# Ethics and Limitations

Developmental psychology research requires special ethical care because it often concerns children, adolescents, families, vulnerable populations, disability, health, trauma, aging, schooling, and social inequality.

## Limitations of this repository

- The data are synthetic.
- The outcome score is not a validated measure.
- The models are simplified.
- No clinical, educational, or policy decisions should be made from these examples.
- The examples do not represent any real person, school, neighborhood, clinic, country, or population.

## Responsible use

Responsible developmental modeling should:

- protect privacy;
- avoid stigmatizing groups;
- distinguish risk from destiny;
- preserve human dignity;
- include domain experts;
- use validated measures when making empirical claims;
- treat children, families, disabled people, and older adults as rights-bearing persons, not merely data points;
- communicate uncertainty clearly.
