# Research Notes

## Article

**Aging, Adaptation, and Development in Later Life**

## Purpose of this code folder

This folder translates the article's developmental argument into a reproducible, multi-language research scaffold. It treats later-life development as adaptation under changing conditions of body, health, functional ability, social support, environmental accessibility, care context, dignity, meaning, and time.

## Core research commitments

1. **Aging remains developmental**  
   Later life is modeled as continuing adaptation rather than developmental silence.

2. **Functional ability is person-environment fit**  
   Function is represented as the relation between intrinsic capacity and environmental support.

3. **Adaptation is active development**  
   Adaptive strategy, compensation, support, and accessibility are modeled as developmental processes.

4. **Health burden is real but not total**  
   Health burden shapes later-life development but does not define the person.

5. **Dignity and care context matter**  
   Dignity support, service access, environmental accessibility, and care context are represented as developmental conditions.

6. **Ethical caution is part of the method**  
   These scripts are synthetic demonstrations, not clinical, elder-care, fall-risk, insurance, or policy prediction tools.

## What makes the folder research-grade

- synthetic panel data with repeated later-life waves;
- person-level, care-context-level, and time-varying variables;
- functional ability, social support, health burden, adaptive strategy, environmental accessibility, dignity support, service access, meaning orientation, functional fit, and adjustment score;
- dynamic later-life adjustment modeling;
- health burden and functional-fit trajectory summaries;
- scenario analysis for support, accessibility, adaptive strategy, dignity, service access, health-burden reduction, and combined support;
- SQL schema and analytical queries;
- Python and R workflows;
- compiled-language validation and summary utilities;
- explicit ethics and reproducibility notes;
- notes in every subdirectory.
