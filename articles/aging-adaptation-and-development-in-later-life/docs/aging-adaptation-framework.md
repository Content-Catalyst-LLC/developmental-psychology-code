# Aging, Adaptation, and Development Framework

## Core constructs

| Construct | Developmental meaning |
|---|---|
| Functional ability | Capacity to do and be what matters in real environments |
| Social support | Relational scaffolding, connection, care, and practical help |
| Health burden | Illness, pain, fatigue, multimorbidity, mobility limits, cognitive strain |
| Adaptive strategy | Compensation, routines, assistive support, environmental modification |
| Meaning orientation | Purpose, life review, values, legacy, psychological integration |
| Environmental accessibility | Housing, mobility, transport, public space, digital and physical access |
| Dignity support | Respect, voice, autonomy, privacy, humane institutional care |
| Service access | Health care, home support, prevention, social programs, transportation |
| Functional fit | Synthetic interaction of personal capacity and environmental support |
| Adjustment score | Synthetic outcome representing later-life developmental adaptation |

## Interpretation

This workflow does not treat aging as the absence of development. It models later life as adaptation under changing conditions of body, support, environment, care, and meaning.
