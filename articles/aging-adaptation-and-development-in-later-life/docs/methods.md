# Methods

## Overview

This workflow models aging, adaptation, and development in later life as a longitudinal process. The synthetic data include older adults observed repeatedly across waves and nested within care/community contexts.

The model includes:

- baseline adjustment;
- functional ability;
- social support;
- health burden;
- adaptive strategy;
- meaning orientation;
- environmental accessibility;
- dignity support;
- service access;
- functional fit;
- adjustment score;
- adaptation profile.

## Conceptual interpretation

### Functional ability

Functional ability is modeled as person-environment fit, not as body capacity alone.

### Health burden

Health burden represents illness, pain, fatigue, functional constraint, or multimorbidity pressure. It is a developmental condition, not the whole person.

### Adaptive strategy

Adaptive strategy represents compensatory practices such as routines, assistive devices, environmental modification, pacing, social support, and care coordination.

### Dignity support

Dignity support represents respectful care, voice, privacy, autonomy, and institutional recognition.

### Care context

Care context represents household, neighborhood, community, care-setting, health-service, or institutional environments.

## Synthetic-data caution

The data are not empirical findings. They are simulated for reproducible demonstration only.
