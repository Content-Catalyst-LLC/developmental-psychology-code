# Research Notes

## Article

**Disability, Neurodivergence, and Development**

## Purpose of this code folder

This folder translates the article's developmental argument into a reproducible, multi-language research scaffold. It treats disability and neurodivergence as relational developmental processes shaped by support, access, barriers, participation, communication, sensory environments, caregiver advocacy, technology, and institutions.

## Core research commitments

1. **Disability is relational**  
   Disability is not only inside the person and not only outside the person. It emerges through embodied difference interacting with support, design, exclusion, access, and time.

2. **Neurodivergence is developmental diversity**  
   Cognitive, attentional, communicative, sensory, and learning differences are part of human development. Support needs are real, but difference should not be reduced to inferiority.

3. **Participation is an outcome**  
   Participation in school, family, peer life, community, and public institutions is modeled directly rather than treated as a side effect.

4. **Barriers are developmental variables**  
   Inaccessible design, sensory overload, communication exclusion, rigid schooling, and service fragmentation shape developmental outcomes.

5. **Ethical caution is part of the method**  
   These scripts are synthetic demonstrations, not diagnostic or eligibility tools.

## What makes the folder research-grade

- synthetic panel data with repeated developmental waves;
- child-level, setting-level, and time-varying variables;
- accessibility, support, barriers, participation, communication access, and advocacy;
- dynamic developmental-outcome modeling;
- access-barrier profile summaries;
- scenario analysis for universal design, communication access, and barrier reduction;
- SQL schema and analytical queries;
- Python and R workflows;
- compiled-language validation and summary utilities;
- explicit ethics and accessibility notes;
- notes in every subdirectory.
