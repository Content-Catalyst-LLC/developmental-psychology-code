# Methods

## Overview

This workflow models developmental psychopathology as a longitudinal pathway system. The synthetic data include children nested within developmental contexts and observed repeatedly across time.

The model includes:

- baseline regulation;
- protective support;
- accumulated risk;
- caregiver stability;
- biological sensitivity;
- community support;
- school belonging;
- service access;
- timing-weighted risk;
- cumulative risk;
- cumulative support;
- adaptation score;
- internalizing score;
- externalizing score;
- risk-support profile.

## Conceptual interpretation

The workflow is designed to represent several core concepts:

### Risk

Risk refers to developmental conditions that increase the probability of maladaptation or distress. Risk is probabilistic, not deterministic.

### Protective support

Protective support includes relational, institutional, school, service, and community conditions that may buffer risk.

### Adaptation

Adaptation is represented as a dynamic score influenced by prior adaptation, current support, current risk, timing-weighted exposure, regulation, stability, and context.

### Multifinality

Internalizing and externalizing scores represent branching pathways from overlapping risk conditions.

### Equifinality

Similar pathway scores may emerge from different combinations of risk, support, regulation, and context.

## Synthetic-data caution

The data are not empirical findings. They are simulated for reproducible demonstration only.
