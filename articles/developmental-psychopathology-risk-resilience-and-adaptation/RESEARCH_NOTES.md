# Research Notes

## Article

**Developmental Psychopathology: Risk, Resilience, and Adaptation**

## Purpose of this code folder

This folder is intended to make the article's conceptual argument operational. It does not merely store sample code. It creates a reusable research scaffold for thinking about developmental psychopathology as a dynamic pathway system.

## Core research commitments

1. **Pathways over labels**  
   Diagnostic descriptions may be useful, but they do not explain developmental history. The workflow focuses on pathways: how adaptation, internalizing tendencies, and externalizing tendencies unfold over time.

2. **Risk is probabilistic, not deterministic**  
   Risk changes probabilities. It does not define a child, family, school, or community.

3. **Resilience is contextual**  
   Resilience is not treated as a heroic individual trait. It is modeled through support, stability, community resources, school belonging, service access, and timing.

4. **Multifinality and equifinality matter**  
   Similar risk exposure can branch into different outcomes. Different histories can converge on similar symptoms.

5. **Ethical caution is part of the method**  
   These scripts are synthetic demonstrations, not clinical tools. The folder includes explicit caution notes to prevent misuse.

## What makes the folder research-grade

- synthetic panel data with repeated developmental waves;
- child-level, context-level, and time-varying variables;
- cumulative exposure measures;
- internalizing and externalizing pathway scores;
- adaptation trajectory modeling;
- scenario analysis;
- SQL schema and analytical queries;
- Python and R workflows;
- compiled-language validation and summary utilities;
- reproducibility and ethics notes;
- folder-level notes in every subdirectory.
