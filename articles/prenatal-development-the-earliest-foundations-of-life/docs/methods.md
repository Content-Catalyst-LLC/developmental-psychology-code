# Methods

## Overview

This workflow models prenatal development as an interacting system of gestational timing, maternal health, prenatal care, nutrition support, social support, chronic stress, toxic exposure, healthcare access, environmental burden, economic security, and early developmental outcome.

The synthetic data include:

- case-level prenatal conditions;
- neighborhood-level contextual conditions;
- effective care as a composite of prenatal care, healthcare access, and social support;
- developmental risk as a composite of chronic stress, toxic exposure, environmental burden, and economic insecurity;
- early developmental outcome as a synthetic newborn or early infancy health-regulation score.

## Conceptual interpretation

### Gestational timing

Gestational weeks represent developmental maturity and timing. In real research, timing is not only a control variable; it is part of prenatal developmental causation.

### Maternal health

Maternal health represents a broad biological and social health condition. It should not be interpreted as a moral score or individual blame variable.

### Effective care

Effective care combines prenatal care, healthcare access, and social support, reflecting the idea that prenatal protection is institutional and relational.

### Developmental risk

Developmental risk combines chronic stress, toxic exposure, environmental burden, and economic insecurity. It represents ecological risk, not individual failure.

### Early outcome

Early outcome is a synthetic measure of newborn or early infancy health-regulation. It is not a clinical measure.

## Synthetic-data caution

The data are not empirical findings. They are simulated for reproducible demonstration only.
