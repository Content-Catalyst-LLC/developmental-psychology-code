# Model Card

## Name

Synthetic prenatal development, risk, protection, and early outcome model.

## Intended use

Research education, reproducible-method scaffolding, prenatal-development scenario demonstration, and conceptual modeling.

## Not intended for

Clinical pregnancy management, fetal diagnosis, obstetric decision-making, maternal-risk scoring, fetal-risk scoring, insurance decisions, benefits decisions, reproductive surveillance, policing, child-welfare referral, or any high-stakes decision-making.

## Inputs

Synthetic variables representing gestational weeks, maternal health, prenatal care, nutrition support, social support, chronic stress, toxic exposure, healthcare access, environmental burden, economic security, effective care, developmental risk, and neighborhood context.

## Outputs

Synthetic early developmental outcomes, stress summaries, effective-care summaries, prenatal case profiles, neighborhood summaries, and public-health scenario comparisons.

## Limitations

- No empirical validation.
- No causal evidence.
- No person-level predictive validity.
- No validated clinical or obstetric instrument.
- Synthetic parameter values.
- Simplified prenatal and neighborhood structure.
- Potential misuse if interpreted as real pregnancy, fetal, or maternal analytics.

## Ethical position

The model treats prenatal development as important while rejecting maternal blame. It should never be used to rank, stigmatize, police, or surveil real pregnant people, infants, families, clinicians, neighborhoods, disabled people, marginalized communities, or health systems.
