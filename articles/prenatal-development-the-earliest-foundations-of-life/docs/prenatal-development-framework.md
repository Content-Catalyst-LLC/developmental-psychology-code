# Prenatal Development Framework

## Core constructs

| Construct | Developmental meaning |
|---|---|
| Gestational weeks | Developmental timing and maturity |
| Maternal health | Biological and social health condition of the pregnant person |
| Prenatal care | Access, regularity, and quality of pregnancy-related care |
| Healthcare access | Neighborhood or system-level care availability |
| Nutrition support | Material support for fetal and maternal health |
| Social support | Relational protection and stress buffering |
| Chronic stress | Ongoing stress burden under pregnancy conditions |
| Toxic exposure | Environmental or chemical exposure burden |
| Environmental burden | Neighborhood-level pollution, unsafe conditions, or ecological strain |
| Economic security | Material stability and reduced scarcity |
| Effective care | Composite care, access, and social support |
| Developmental risk | Composite risk from stress, toxic exposure, environmental burden, and insecurity |
| Early outcome | Synthetic newborn or early infancy developmental health-regulation score |

## Interpretation

This workflow does not treat prenatal development as destiny. It models prenatal development as an early pathway shaped by timing, maternal health, care, exposure, support, and public systems.
