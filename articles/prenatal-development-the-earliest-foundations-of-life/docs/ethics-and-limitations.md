# Ethics and Limitations

## Not a clinical, obstetric, fetal-risk, maternal-risk, insurance, or surveillance tool

This repository must not be used for diagnosis, pregnancy management, fetal-risk scoring, maternal-risk scoring, insurance decisions, reproductive surveillance, benefits decisions, policing, child-welfare referral, or any high-stakes decision.

## Key risks

- reducing pregnant people to risk scores;
- turning prenatal-development science into maternal blame;
- ignoring healthcare access, environmental injustice, poverty, racism, disability, violence, housing, work conditions, and social support;
- mistaking synthetic output for empirical evidence;
- ranking pregnancies, neighborhoods, clinicians, hospitals, or communities;
- using prenatal risk language to justify surveillance rather than support;
- treating prenatal conditions as destiny and ignoring postnatal support, care, intervention, and resilience.

## Responsible use

Use this repository to teach and explore:

- prenatal development as the first developmental environment;
- timing, vulnerability, and sensitive periods;
- prenatal risk and protection;
- public-health support and prenatal care;
- social and environmental conditions of pregnancy;
- synthetic longitudinal and ecological modeling.

Responsible real-world work requires validated measures, clinical and public-health expertise, privacy protection, reproductive autonomy, informed consent, careful causal design, institutional review, anti-bias safeguards, disability rights awareness, environmental justice, and respect for maternal dignity.
