# Research Notes

## Article

**Prenatal Development: The Earliest Foundations of Life**

## Purpose of this code folder

This folder translates the article's developmental argument into a reproducible, multi-language research scaffold. It treats prenatal development as the first developmental environment: a biological process nested within maternal health, prenatal care, nutrition, stress, placental exchange, toxic exposure, neighborhood conditions, healthcare access, environmental burden, public health, and inequality.

## Core research commitments

1. **Development begins before birth**  
   Prenatal development is part of developmental psychology because timing, vulnerability, plasticity, and context are already operative before infancy.

2. **Prenatal development is ecological**  
   Maternal health, fetal development, prenatal care, neighborhood conditions, environmental exposure, and social support form an interacting developmental system.

3. **Risk and protection are interactive**  
   Chronic stress, toxic exposure, poor nutrition, and environmental burden do not act alone; effective care and social support may buffer some risks.

4. **Timing is part of causation**  
   Gestational timing and sensitive periods matter because different developmental processes have different windows of vulnerability.

5. **Inequality is embodied early**  
   Prenatal development is shaped by healthcare access, food security, environmental justice, housing, work conditions, racism, violence, and social protection.

6. **Ethical caution is part of the method**  
   These scripts are synthetic demonstrations, not clinical, obstetric, insurance, surveillance, reproductive-control, or maternal-blame tools.

## What makes the folder research-grade

- synthetic prenatal data with gestational timing and contextual structure;
- case-level and neighborhood-level variables;
- maternal health, prenatal care, nutrition support, social support, chronic stress, toxic exposure, environmental burden, healthcare access, economic security, effective care, developmental risk, and early outcome;
- risk-protection interaction modeling;
- neighborhood contextual summaries;
- prenatal public-health scenario analysis;
- SQL schema and analytical queries;
- Python and R workflows;
- compiled-language validation and summary utilities;
- explicit ethics and reproducibility notes;
- notes in every subdirectory.
