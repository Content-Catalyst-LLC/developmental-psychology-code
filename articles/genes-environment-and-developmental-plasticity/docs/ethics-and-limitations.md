# Ethics and Limitations

## Not a genetic, clinical, or policy prediction tool

This repository must not be used for genetic interpretation, diagnosis, child-risk scoring, service eligibility, school placement, clinical triage, insurance decisions, surveillance, or any high-stakes decision.

## Key risks

- treating biological sensitivity as destiny;
- reducing children to genetic or biological profiles;
- ignoring caregiving, poverty, nutrition, pollution, schooling, racism, disability, and institutional context;
- mistaking synthetic output for empirical evidence;
- using gene-environment logic to blame families or excuse unequal environments;
- overclaiming epigenetic or biological embedding mechanisms;
- ranking individuals or groups by simulated susceptibility.

## Responsible use

Use this repository to teach and explore:

- gene-environment interaction;
- biological sensitivity and environmental responsiveness;
- developmental timing and sensitive-period assumptions;
- biological embedding as a conceptual model;
- synthetic longitudinal modeling;
- ethical caution in computational developmental science.

Responsible real-world work requires validated measures, domain expertise, consent, privacy protection, careful causal design, institutional review, and explicit attention to social inequality.
