# Model Card

## Name

Synthetic genes, environment, and developmental plasticity model.

## Intended use

Research education, reproducible-method scaffolding, developmental-plasticity scenario demonstration, and conceptual modeling.

## Not intended for

Genetic interpretation, clinical diagnosis, child-risk scoring, intervention eligibility, education placement, insurance decisions, surveillance, or any high-stakes decision-making.

## Inputs

Synthetic variables representing biological sensitivity, caregiving quality, environmental stress, nutritional support, early exposure, intervention support, school support, neighborhood safety, service access, and timing-sensitive exposure.

## Outputs

Synthetic development scores, embedded exposure summaries, and sensitivity-stress profiles.

## Limitations

- No empirical validation.
- No causal evidence.
- No genetic validity.
- No child-level predictive validity.
- Synthetic parameter values.
- Simplified biology and environment structure.
- Potential misuse if interpreted as real developmental analytics.

## Ethical position

The model treats genes and environment as developmental coaction, not as destiny or blame. It should never be used to rank or stigmatize real children, families, schools, neighborhoods, or groups.
