# Research Notes

## Article

**Genes, Environment, and Developmental Plasticity**

## Purpose of this code folder

This folder translates the article's developmental argument into a reproducible, multi-language research scaffold. It treats development as coaction among biological sensitivity, environmental context, timing, accumulated exposure, and support.

## Core research commitments

1. **Genes are developmental, not destiny**  
   Genetic and biological differences are modeled as sensitivity and potential that operate through context, timing, and regulation.

2. **Environment is developmental condition**  
   Caregiving, stress, nutrition, school support, neighborhood safety, service access, and intervention support are modeled as developmental conditions, not background variables.

3. **Plasticity links biology and lived context**  
   Plasticity is represented through timing weights, biological embedding, cumulative stress, cumulative support, and interaction effects.

4. **Timing matters**  
   Early and repeated exposures are represented with timing-sensitive weights to illustrate sensitive-period logic.

5. **Ethical caution is part of the method**  
   These scripts are synthetic demonstrations, not genetic, clinical, educational, or policy prediction tools.

## What makes the folder research-grade

- synthetic panel data with repeated developmental waves;
- child-level, context-level, and time-varying variables;
- biological sensitivity, caregiving, stress, nutrition, school support, neighborhood safety, service access, and intervention support;
- timing-sensitive exposure and embedded stress/support states;
- dynamic developmental-outcome modeling;
- sensitivity-stress profile summaries;
- scenario analysis for care, nutrition, school support, and stress reduction;
- SQL schema and analytical queries;
- Python and R workflows;
- compiled-language validation and summary utilities;
- explicit ethics and reproducibility notes;
- notes in every subdirectory.
