# Trauma-Informed Developmental Systems

A trauma-informed developmental system does not simply ask what is wrong with a child or family. It asks what burdens a person has had to adapt to, what safety has been missing, what supports are available, and what institutional practices may be compounding harm.

## System dimensions

| Dimension | Developmental purpose |
|---|---|
| Safety | Reduce threat and unpredictability |
| Predictability | Support regulation and trust |
| Relationship | Provide co-regulation and belonging |
| Agency | Avoid reenacting helplessness |
| Accountability | Repair harm without humiliation |
| Material support | Stabilize housing, care, food, transportation, and services |
| Cultural humility | Avoid imposing one narrow model of normality |
| Institutional reflexivity | Examine how systems reproduce trauma |

## Practice note

Trauma-informed work is not permissiveness and not the absence of boundaries. It is a disciplined approach to safety, dignity, regulation, learning, accountability, and repair.
