# Ethics and Limitations

Trauma and adversity modeling requires special care.

## Key risks

- Treating trauma as destiny.
- Reducing people to adverse experiences.
- Treating family or community burden as individual failure.
- Ignoring poverty, violence, discrimination, displacement, and institutional neglect.
- Ranking children or communities with simplistic risk scores.
- Using synthetic demonstrations as clinical evidence.
- Using models without privacy protection, consent, and domain expertise.

## Responsible framing

Responsible trauma and adversity analysis should:

- distinguish risk from destiny;
- include protective factors and repair;
- avoid deficit language;
- preserve attention to institutions and material conditions;
- involve trauma-informed practitioners and community expertise in real studies;
- avoid clinical claims without validated measures;
- protect privacy and safety;
- communicate uncertainty clearly.
