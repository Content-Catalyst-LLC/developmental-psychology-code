# Research Notes

## Article
Attachment, Caregiving, and Early Emotional Development

## Research frame

This repository operationalizes attachment as a dynamic relational-developmental process. It treats early emotional regulation as shaped by caregiving responsiveness, relational repair, caregiver support, childcare continuity, neighborhood safety, family service access, caregiving ecology, infant temperament, disability support need, chronic stress, and repeated experiences of co-regulation.

## Core constructs

- regulation score;
- baseline regulation;
- caregiving quality;
- relational repair;
- caregiver support;
- childcare continuity;
- neighborhood safety;
- family service access;
- caregiving ecology support;
- chronic stress;
- current stress;
- temperament reactivity;
- disability support need;
- caregiving support context;
- attachment-development profile.

## Research-grade elements

- synthetic longitudinal early childhood panel data;
- context-level caregiving ecology metadata;
- dynamic state-dependence modeling;
- multilevel caregiving-context modeling;
- scenario comparison for caregiving support, repair, childcare continuity, service access, stress reduction, and combined support;
- SQL schema and analytical queries;
- Python and R workflows;
- compiled-language validation and summary utilities;
- reproducibility, validation, responsible-use, and model-card documentation.
