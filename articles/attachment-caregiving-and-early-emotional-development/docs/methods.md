# Methods

## Overview

This workflow creates a synthetic longitudinal panel for attachment, caregiving, and early emotional-development research. It demonstrates how caregiving responsiveness, relational repair, caregiver support, childcare continuity, neighborhood safety, service access, caregiving ecology, stress, temperament, and disability support can be represented across repeated developmental waves.

## Design

The synthetic panel includes:

- child-level baseline regulation;
- repeated observation waves;
- context-level caregiving ecology variables;
- time-varying caregiving responsiveness, repair, caregiver support, and stress;
- temperament reactivity and disability support need;
- caregiving support context;
- broad early emotional regulation outcome;
- attachment-development profiles.

## Modeling strategy

The repository uses synthetic data generation, dynamic regression, multilevel modeling, scenario comparison, SQL summaries, and compiled-language validation utilities.

## Interpretation

The models illustrate attachment as a developmental process shaped by co-regulation, repair, care quality, stress, fit, support systems, and social ecology rather than a static individual label.
