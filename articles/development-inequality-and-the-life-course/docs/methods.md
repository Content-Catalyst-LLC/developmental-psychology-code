# Methods Note

This workflow models life-course inequality as a repeated-observation process.

## Main concepts

| Concept | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Resources | Food security, health access, stable housing, school quality, time, opportunity |
| Burdens | Stress, instability, exclusion, environmental risk, institutional neglect |
| Supports | Family, school, community, service, policy, and institutional buffering |
| Timing | Earlier or transitional periods may carry stronger developmental influence |
| Context | Neighborhood, school, clinic, family-service ecology, or policy environment |

## Design

The synthetic panel observes people repeatedly across developmental time. It uses:

1. person-level baseline variation;
2. context-level opportunity/support/safety;
3. time-varying resources and burdens;
4. timing-weighted cumulative exposure;
5. dynamic dependence on prior developmental state.
