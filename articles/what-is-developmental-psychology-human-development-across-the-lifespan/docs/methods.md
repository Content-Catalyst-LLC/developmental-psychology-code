# Methods

## Overview

This workflow creates a synthetic longitudinal panel for developmental psychology. It is designed to demonstrate how developmental trajectories can be represented across repeated waves and nested contexts.

## Design

The synthetic panel includes:

- child-level baseline characteristics;
- repeated developmental observation waves;
- school/context-level conditions;
- time-varying stress and support;
- intervention exposure;
- disability support need and accommodation;
- structural risk;
- lagged outcome structure;
- developmental trajectory profiles.

## Modeling strategy

The repository uses three complementary approaches:

1. **Synthetic data generation** to make assumptions transparent.
2. **Dynamic regression** to estimate state dependence and time-varying context.
3. **Multilevel modeling** to represent clustering within schools or developmental institutions.

## Interpretation

The models illustrate developmental concepts: timing, trajectory, accumulated risk, support, institutional context, intervention, and feedback. They are scaffolds for reasoning, teaching, and methodological extension.
