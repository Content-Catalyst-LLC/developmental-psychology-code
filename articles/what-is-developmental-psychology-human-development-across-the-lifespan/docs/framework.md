# Developmental Psychology Framework

## Core developmental claims

1. Development is temporal: individual functioning must be interpreted across time.
2. Development is relational: caregiver, peer, family, school, and community contexts matter.
3. Development is embodied: cognitive, emotional, behavioral, and biological processes are linked.
4. Development is institutional: schools, clinics, welfare systems, and public policy shape pathways.
5. Development is unequal: structural risk and access to support alter opportunity.
6. Development is probabilistic: early experience matters without fully determining the future.
7. Development is modifiable: supportive contexts and interventions can redirect pathways.

## Analytical structure

A general developmental trajectory can be represented as:

```text
Y_it = alpha_i + beta_i * t + gamma * X_it + delta * Z_i + error_it
```

Where:

- `Y_it` = developmental outcome for person `i` at time `t`;
- `alpha_i` = starting point;
- `beta_i` = growth rate;
- `X_it` = time-varying conditions;
- `Z_i` = stable or slowly changing background conditions.

The synthetic data generated here translate that logic into reproducible workflows.
