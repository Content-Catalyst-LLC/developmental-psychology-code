# Model Card

## Name

Synthetic stage-like development, threshold, and transition-readiness model.

## Intended use

Research education, reproducible-method scaffolding, stage-theory critique, threshold modeling, and scenario demonstration.

## Not intended for

Developmental screening, maturity scoring, clinical diagnosis, school placement, disability classification, student tracking, child ranking, eligibility decisions, surveillance, or any high-stakes decision-making.

## Inputs

Synthetic variables representing baseline functioning, growth rate, threshold timing, stage-like pattern, family support, current support, chronic stress, school support, resource stability, transition readiness, and developmental outcome.

## Outputs

Synthetic developmental trajectories, stage-pattern summaries, threshold summaries, transition-readiness summaries, and scenario comparisons.

## Limitations

- No empirical validation.
- No causal evidence.
- No person-level predictive validity.
- No validated clinical, educational, or developmental instrument.
- Synthetic parameter values.
- Simplified context and threshold structure.
- Potential misuse if interpreted as real developmental analytics.

## Ethical position

The model treats stage theory as a conceptual and methodological object, not as a ranking system for real people. It should never be used to classify or rank children, adolescents, adults, cultures, disabled people, neurodivergent people, schools, families, or communities.
