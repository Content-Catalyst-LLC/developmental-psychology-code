# Methods

## Overview

This workflow models stage-like development as a combination of continuous growth, threshold transitions, logistic transition, contextual support, chronic stress, resource stability, and transition readiness.

The synthetic data include:

- repeated developmental waves;
- person-level baseline functioning and growth rate;
- individual threshold timing;
- stage-pattern assignment;
- family support and time-varying support;
- chronic stress;
- context-level school support and resource stability;
- transition readiness;
- stage-like and logistic transition components;
- developmental outcome scores.

## Conceptual interpretation

### Continuous growth

Continuous growth represents incremental developmental change over time.

### Stage-like transition

Stage-like transition represents a threshold shift after an individual reaches a transition time.

### Logistic transition

Logistic transition represents smoother reorganization, where visible change accelerates around a threshold and then stabilizes.

### Transition readiness

Transition readiness combines current support, context support, and resource stability while subtracting chronic stress burden.

### Context

Context represents school, family, neighborhood, clinic, or institutional environment.

## Synthetic-data caution

The data are not empirical findings. They are simulated for reproducible demonstration only.
