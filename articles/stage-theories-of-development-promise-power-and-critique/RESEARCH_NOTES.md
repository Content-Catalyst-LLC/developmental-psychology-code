# Research Notes

## Article

**Stage Theories of Development: Promise, Power, and Critique**

## Purpose of this code folder

This folder translates the article's developmental argument into a reproducible, multi-language research scaffold. It treats stage theories as claims about the shape of developmental change: continuous growth, threshold shifts, logistic transitions, and context-sensitive reorganization.

## Core research commitments

1. **Stage theories are heuristic, not destiny**  
   Stage models help identify qualitative reorganization, but they should not become rigid age scripts.

2. **Thresholds can be modeled without universalizing them**  
   Transitions can vary across persons, contexts, and developmental domains.

3. **Context alters transition timing and meaning**  
   Family support, school support, resource stability, and chronic stress can change visible developmental trajectories.

4. **Development can be continuous in process and discontinuous in appearance**  
   Stage-like outcomes may emerge from many smaller cumulative processes.

5. **Culture, inequality, disability, and neurodivergence matter**  
   Standard stage sequences should not be used to rank people against narrow developmental ideals.

6. **Ethical caution is part of the method**  
   These scripts are synthetic demonstrations, not screening, ranking, placement, clinical, or high-stakes decision tools.

## What makes the folder research-grade

- synthetic longitudinal data with repeated waves;
- child/person-level, context-level, and time-varying variables;
- continuous growth, stage-like threshold shifts, logistic transitions, transition readiness, support, stress, resource stability, and contextual clustering;
- dynamic modeling with lagged outcomes;
- threshold and transition-readiness interaction modeling;
- scenario analysis for support, stress reduction, resource stability, and transition readiness;
- SQL schema and analytical queries;
- Python and R workflows;
- compiled-language validation and summary utilities;
- explicit ethics and reproducibility notes;
- notes in every subdirectory.
