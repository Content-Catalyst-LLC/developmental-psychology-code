# Methods

## Overview

This workflow models temperament and individual differences as a longitudinal developmental process. The synthetic data include children observed repeatedly across waves and nested within classroom contexts.

The model includes:

- temperament reactivity;
- inhibition;
- activity level;
- baseline adjustment;
- chronic stress;
- family support;
- school fit;
- classroom structure;
- teacher responsiveness;
- movement flexibility;
- acute stress;
- accommodation;
- goodness of fit;
- developmental adjustment score;
- temperament profile.

## Conceptual interpretation

### Temperament reactivity

Temperament reactivity represents early arousal, emotional intensity, sensitivity, or responsiveness to stimulation and stress.

### Inhibition

Inhibition represents behavioral caution, withdrawal, hesitancy, or slow approach under novelty.

### Activity level

Activity level represents movement, energy, restlessness, approach, and physical engagement.

### Goodness of fit

Goodness of fit represents the alignment between child characteristics and environmental support, demand, accommodation, teacher responsiveness, and classroom flexibility.

### Adjustment score

Adjustment score is a broad synthetic outcome representing socioemotional adjustment, self-regulation, participation, or developmental functioning.

## Synthetic-data caution

The data are not empirical findings. They are simulated for reproducible demonstration only.
