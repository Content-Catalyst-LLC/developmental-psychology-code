# Temperament and Individual Differences Framework

## Core constructs

| Construct | Developmental meaning |
|---|---|
| Temperament reactivity | Intensity and speed of emotional or physiological response |
| Inhibition | Caution, slow approach, withdrawal, or hesitancy under novelty |
| Activity level | Movement, energy, exploration, restlessness, physical engagement |
| Family support | Co-regulation, routines, emotional safety, caregiver responsiveness |
| School fit | Match between child style and school expectations or supports |
| Chronic stress | Repeated adversity, instability, or environmental strain |
| Acute stress | Time-varying stress exposure or dysregulating event load |
| Accommodation | Adjustments that improve fit and participation |
| Teacher responsiveness | Classroom relational support and interpretation of behavior |
| Movement flexibility | Environmental support for active, sensory, or embodied learning |
| Goodness of fit | Synthetic measure of child-environment match |
| Adjustment score | Synthetic outcome representing socioemotional adjustment and participation |

## Interpretation

This workflow does not treat temperament as destiny. It models temperament as early difference whose developmental consequences depend on support, stress, fit, accommodation, interpretation, and institutional design.
