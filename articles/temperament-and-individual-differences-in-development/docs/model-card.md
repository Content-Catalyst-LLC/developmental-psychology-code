# Model Card

## Name

Synthetic temperament, individual differences, and goodness-of-fit model.

## Intended use

Research education, reproducible-method scaffolding, temperament-context scenario demonstration, and conceptual modeling.

## Not intended for

Clinical diagnosis, disability screening, mental-health triage, school discipline, special education placement, child-welfare decisions, risk scoring, surveillance, or any high-stakes decision-making.

## Inputs

Synthetic variables representing temperament reactivity, inhibition, activity level, family support, school fit, stress exposure, accommodation, teacher responsiveness, movement flexibility, classroom structure, and goodness of fit.

## Outputs

Synthetic adjustment scores, goodness-of-fit summaries, temperament trajectories, child-profile summaries, and scenario comparisons.

## Limitations

- No empirical validation.
- No causal evidence.
- No person-level predictive validity.
- No validated clinical or educational instrument.
- Synthetic parameter values.
- Simplified temperament and classroom structure.
- Potential misuse if interpreted as real child analytics.

## Ethical position

The model treats children as developing persons with dignity, individuality, rights, needs, context, and possibility. It should never be used to rank or stigmatize real children, families, caregivers, teachers, schools, disabled children, neurodivergent children, or communities.
