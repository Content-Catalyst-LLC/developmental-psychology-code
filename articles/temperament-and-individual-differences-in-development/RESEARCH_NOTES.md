# Research Notes

## Article

**Temperament and Individual Differences in Development**

## Purpose of this code folder

This folder translates the article's developmental argument into a reproducible, multi-language research scaffold. It treats temperament as an early developmental contour whose consequences depend on caregiving, co-regulation, classroom fit, culture, stress, support, accommodation, neurodevelopment, and institutional interpretation.

## Core research commitments

1. **Temperament is early individuality, not destiny**  
   Temperamental reactivity, inhibition, activity, and sensitivity shape developmental pathways but do not determine them.

2. **Goodness of fit is central**  
   Developmental outcomes depend on the match between child characteristics and environmental demands, supports, and accommodations.

3. **Regulation is co-produced**  
   Children do not begin with fully mature self-regulation. Co-regulation, routines, safety, and relational predictability matter.

4. **Schools and institutions interpret temperament**  
   Classroom design, teacher responsiveness, movement flexibility, disciplinary practice, and institutional patience influence outcomes.

5. **Culture, inequality, and neurodivergence matter**  
   Temperament is interpreted through cultural norms, disability categories, racialized/gendered/classed expectations, and institutional bias.

6. **Ethical caution is part of the method**  
   These scripts are synthetic demonstrations, not clinical, educational-placement, discipline, disability-screening, or risk-prediction tools.

## What makes the folder research-grade

- synthetic panel data with repeated developmental waves;
- child-level, classroom-level, and time-varying variables;
- temperament reactivity, inhibition, activity level, caregiver support, classroom fit, stress exposure, accommodation, teacher responsiveness, movement flexibility, goodness of fit, and adjustment;
- dynamic developmental modeling with lagged outcomes;
- temperament-context interaction modeling;
- goodness-of-fit and scenario summaries;
- SQL schema and analytical queries;
- Python and R workflows;
- compiled-language validation and summary utilities;
- explicit ethics and reproducibility notes;
- notes in every subdirectory.
