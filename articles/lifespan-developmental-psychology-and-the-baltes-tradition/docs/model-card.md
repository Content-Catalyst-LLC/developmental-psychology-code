# Model Card

## Name

Synthetic lifespan developmental psychology and Baltes tradition model.

## Intended use

Research education, reproducible-method scaffolding, lifespan-development scenario demonstration, and conceptual modeling.

## Not intended for

Clinical diagnosis, cognitive screening, elder-care eligibility, benefits decisions, insurance decisions, employment decisions, health-risk scoring, surveillance, or any high-stakes decision-making.

## Inputs

Synthetic variables representing gains, losses, plasticity, contextual support, compensation, health resources, selection, optimization, cohort context, institutional security, and historical support.

## Outputs

Synthetic development scores, SOC summaries, gains-losses trajectories, and adaptation-profile groupings.

## Limitations

- No empirical validation.
- No causal evidence.
- No person-level predictive validity.
- Synthetic parameter values.
- Simplified lifespan and cohort structure.
- Potential misuse if interpreted as real developmental analytics.

## Ethical position

The model treats lifespan development as lifelong, multidirectional, and contextual. It should never be used to rank or stigmatize real people, cohorts, older adults, disabled people, families, communities, or institutions.
