# Methods

## Overview

This workflow models lifespan developmental psychology in the Baltes tradition as a longitudinal process. The synthetic data include people observed repeatedly across lifespan waves and nested within cohort contexts.

The model includes:

- gains;
- losses;
- plasticity;
- contextual support;
- compensatory capacity;
- health resource;
- selection;
- optimization;
- compensation;
- SOC index;
- historical support;
- institutional security;
- development score;
- adaptation profile.

## Conceptual interpretation

### Gains and losses

Gains and losses are modeled separately so the workflow does not collapse development into one upward or downward line.

### Plasticity

Plasticity represents openness to change under supportive conditions. It is not unlimited malleability.

### Contextual support

Contextual support represents interpersonal, institutional, material, educational, or social support.

### Selective optimization with compensation

Selection, optimization, and compensation are modeled as strategic adaptation processes.

### Cohort context

Historical support and institutional security represent shared context across cohorts.

## Synthetic-data caution

The data are not empirical findings. They are simulated for reproducible demonstration only.
