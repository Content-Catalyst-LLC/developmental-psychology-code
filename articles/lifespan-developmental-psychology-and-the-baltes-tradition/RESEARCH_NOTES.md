# Research Notes

## Article

**Lifespan Developmental Psychology and the Baltes Tradition**

## Purpose of this code folder

This folder translates the article's developmental argument into a reproducible, multi-language research scaffold. It treats lifespan development as lifelong, multidirectional, multidimensional, historically embedded, and shaped by gains, losses, plasticity, support, and strategic adaptation.

## Core research commitments

1. **Development is lifelong**  
   Development is modeled across repeated waves rather than as a childhood-only process.

2. **Development is multidirectional**  
   Gains and losses are modeled separately so development is not reduced to one upward or downward curve.

3. **Plasticity has limits and conditions**  
   Plasticity is represented as responsiveness to support, intervention, and context, not unlimited malleability.

4. **Adaptation is strategic**  
   Selection, optimization, and compensation are represented as explicit adaptation processes.

5. **Lives are historically embedded**  
   Cohort context, institutional security, and support conditions are represented as shared contextual influences.

6. **Ethical caution is part of the method**  
   These scripts are synthetic demonstrations, not clinical, cognitive, insurance, employment, or policy prediction tools.

## What makes the folder research-grade

- synthetic panel data with repeated lifespan waves;
- person-level, cohort-level, and time-varying variables;
- gains, losses, plasticity, contextual support, compensation, health resource, selection, optimization, and cohort context;
- dynamic developmental-outcome modeling;
- SOC index and adaptation-profile summaries;
- scenario analysis for support, compensation, institutional security, health resource, and loss reduction;
- SQL schema and analytical queries;
- Python and R workflows;
- compiled-language validation and summary utilities;
- explicit ethics and reproducibility notes;
- notes in every subdirectory.
