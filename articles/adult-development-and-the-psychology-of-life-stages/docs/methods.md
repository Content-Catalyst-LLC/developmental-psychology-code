# Methods

## Overview

This workflow models adult development and the psychology of life stages as a longitudinal process. The synthetic data include adults observed repeatedly across waves and nested within contexts that stand in for workplaces, households, neighborhoods, care systems, or community settings.

The model includes:

- baseline adjustment;
- life stage;
- relational support;
- work integration;
- health burden;
- adaptive resources;
- role burden;
- institutional support;
- community stability;
- adjustment score;
- adult development profile.

## Conceptual interpretation

### Life stage

Life stage is represented as a contextual marker: young adulthood, midlife, and later adulthood. It is not treated as a rigid universal sequence or normative script.

### Relational support

Relational support represents intimate, family, friendship, caregiving, and community support.

### Work integration

Work integration represents meaningful labor, institutional recognition, role coherence, or economic participation. It does not imply that paid work is the only source of adult meaning.

### Role burden

Role burden represents care load, work strain, family obligation, financial stress, institutional demand, or role overload.

### Adaptive resources

Adaptive resources represent coping capacity, social resources, time flexibility, problem-solving supports, and context-specific forms of resilience.

## Synthetic-data caution

The data are not empirical findings. They are simulated for reproducible demonstration only.
