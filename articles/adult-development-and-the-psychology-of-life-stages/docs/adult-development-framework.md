# Adult Development and Life Stages Framework

## Core constructs

| Construct | Developmental meaning |
|---|---|
| Life stage | Young adulthood, midlife, and later adulthood as contextual pressures |
| Relational support | Intimacy, family, friendship, care, community, social connection |
| Work integration | Meaningful labor, institutional role, economic participation, recognition |
| Health burden | Illness, pain, fatigue, stress load, disability, embodied constraint |
| Adaptive resources | Coping capacity, support, time flexibility, accommodations, resilience |
| Role burden | Caregiving, work strain, family obligation, financial stress, overload |
| Institutional support | Systems that protect, recognize, or burden adults |
| Community stability | Social context, neighborhood support, civic life, local continuity |
| Adjustment score | Synthetic outcome representing adult developmental adaptation |

## Interpretation

This workflow does not treat adulthood as fixed maturity. It models adulthood as ongoing development under changing conditions of role, body, relationship, institution, care, work, health, and time.
