# Research Notes

## Article

**Adult Development and the Psychology of Life Stages**

## Purpose of this code folder

This folder translates the article's developmental argument into a reproducible, multi-language research scaffold. It treats adult development as relational, embodied, institutional, cumulative, and historically shaped rather than as stable maturity or decline-only aging.

## Core research commitments

1. **Adulthood remains developmental**  
   Adult lives are modeled as continuing change across identity, work, intimacy, care, health, meaning, and time.

2. **Life stages are contexts, not scripts**  
   Young adulthood, midlife, and later adulthood are represented as stage-linked contexts rather than universal obligations.

3. **Adult development is institutional**  
   Work, community, healthcare, family systems, and social support shape adult adjustment.

4. **Role burden and care matter**  
   Adult development is not only self-actualization; it includes caregiving, role overload, responsibility, and strain.

5. **Health burden is embodied development**  
   Health burden shapes adult change but does not define the person.

6. **Ethical caution is part of the method**  
   These scripts are synthetic demonstrations, not workplace, clinical, insurance, benefits, or mental-health prediction tools.

## What makes the folder research-grade

- synthetic panel data with repeated adult-development waves;
- person-level, context-level, life-stage, and time-varying variables;
- relational support, work integration, health burden, adaptive resources, role burden, institutional support, community stability, life stage, and adjustment score;
- dynamic adult-adjustment modeling;
- life-stage and context trajectory summaries;
- scenario analysis for support, work, adaptive resources, health burden, role burden, institutional support, and combined adult-development support;
- SQL schema and analytical queries;
- Python and R workflows;
- compiled-language validation and summary utilities;
- explicit ethics and reproducibility notes;
- notes in every subdirectory.
