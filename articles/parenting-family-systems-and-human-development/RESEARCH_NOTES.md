# Research Notes

## Article

**Parenting, Family Systems, and Human Development**

## Purpose of this code folder

This folder translates the article's developmental argument into a reproducible, multi-language research scaffold. It treats parenting as a relational developmental system rather than a set of isolated adult behaviors.

## Core research commitments

1. **Parenting is relational development**  
   Parenting is modeled as recurring responsiveness, co-regulation, guidance, repair, boundary-setting, and emotional organization.

2. **Families are systems**  
   Development is shaped by household stability, kin support, sibling support, family climate, caregiver stress, and repeated relational patterns.

3. **Stress is ecological**  
   Stress is not treated as a private parental failure. It is modeled as a household and contextual condition that changes developmental bandwidth.

4. **Caregiver support matters**  
   Supporting caregivers is part of supporting children. Caregiver support, respite, kin support, and household stability are modeled as developmental supports.

5. **Ethical caution is part of the method**  
   These scripts are synthetic demonstrations, not parenting evaluations, custody tools, diagnostic systems, or family-risk prediction engines.

## What makes the folder research-grade

- synthetic panel data with repeated developmental waves;
- child-level, household-level, and time-varying variables;
- parenting responsiveness, family climate, stress, household stability, kin support, sibling support, and caregiver support;
- dynamic developmental-outcome modeling;
- family-support profile summaries;
- scenario analysis for caregiver support, stress reduction, kin support, and household stability;
- SQL schema and analytical queries;
- Python and R workflows;
- compiled-language validation and summary utilities;
- explicit ethics and reproducibility notes;
- notes in every subdirectory.
