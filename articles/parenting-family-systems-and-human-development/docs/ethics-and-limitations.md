# Ethics and Limitations

## Not a parenting or family-risk scoring system

This repository must not be used for custody decisions, family surveillance, child protective prediction, parenting evaluation, diagnosis, clinical triage, resource denial, or any high-stakes decision.

## Key risks

- reducing families to scores;
- treating child outcomes as parental merit or failure alone;
- ignoring poverty, work conditions, housing, health, disability, racism, language access, and institutional burden;
- using synthetic output as evidence;
- ranking caregivers, households, children, or communities;
- turning ecological stress into moral blame;
- ignoring abuse, neglect, and safety concerns where protection is necessary.

## Responsible use

Use this repository to teach and explore:

- parenting as relational development;
- family systems and ecological context;
- caregiver support and stress reduction;
- synthetic longitudinal modeling;
- multilevel household structure;
- ethical caution in computational family research.

Responsible real-world work requires validated measures, consent, privacy protection, careful causal design, institutional review, trauma-informed practice, and attention to structural inequality.
