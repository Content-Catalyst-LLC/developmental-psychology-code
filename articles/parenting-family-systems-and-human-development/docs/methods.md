# Methods

## Overview

This workflow models parenting and family systems as longitudinal developmental processes. The synthetic data include children nested within households and observed repeatedly across time.

The model includes:

- parenting responsiveness;
- family climate;
- external stress;
- sibling support;
- child regulation;
- caregiver support;
- household stability;
- kin support;
- economic security;
- family support index;
- development score;
- family-support profile.

## Conceptual interpretation

### Parenting responsiveness

Parenting responsiveness represents recurring caregiving attunement, support, guidance, and response to child needs.

### Family climate

Family climate represents relational tone, communication, warmth, conflict, stability, and emotional safety.

### Household stability

Household stability represents routine, predictability, housing/household continuity, and organized caregiving context.

### Kin support

Kin support represents extended-family or community support available to the household.

### Stress

Stress represents external strain, conflict burden, material pressure, caregiver load, or chronic family-system strain.

### Caregiver support

Caregiver support represents family-support intervention, coaching, respite, concrete support, or relational support for caregivers.

## Synthetic-data caution

The data are not empirical findings. They are simulated for reproducible demonstration only.
