# Research Notes

## Article
Social Development, Peer Relations, and the Formation of the Self

## Research frame

This repository operationalizes social development as a longitudinal process in which the social self is shaped by peer support, friendship quality, family support, school connectedness, teacher support, social interpretation, inclusion climate, restorative practice, exclusion, bullying exposure, digital comparison, and repeated experiences of recognition or misrecognition.

## Core constructs

- social-self score;
- peer support;
- friendship quality;
- family support;
- social interpretation skill;
- school connectedness;
- teacher support;
- anti-bullying climate;
- inclusion climate;
- restorative practice access;
- chronic exclusion;
- current exclusion;
- bullying exposure;
- digital comparison stress;
- social support context;
- social-development profile.

## Research-grade elements

- synthetic longitudinal child/adolescent panel data;
- school/context metadata;
- social-self and connectedness trajectories;
- dynamic state-dependence modeling;
- multilevel social-development modeling;
- scenario comparison for social supports and social risks;
- SQL schema and analytical queries;
- Python and R workflows;
- compiled-language validation and summary utilities;
- reproducibility, validation, responsible-use, and model-card documentation.
