# Model Card

## Name

Synthetic social development and peer relations trajectory model.

## Intended use

Research education, article companion materials, methods teaching, longitudinal analysis practice, social-development modeling, and reproducible workflow development.

## Inputs

Synthetic variables representing baseline social functioning, peer support, friendship quality, family support, social interpretation skill, school connectedness, teacher support, anti-bullying climate, inclusion climate, restorative practice access, chronic exclusion, current exclusion, bullying exposure, digital comparison stress, social support context, and social-self score.

## Outputs

Longitudinal synthetic panel, profile summaries, school/context summaries, dynamic regression outputs, trajectory plots, scenario comparisons, and validation summaries.

## Data notes

All generated data are synthetic and parameterized for transparency. The design is suitable for demonstration and extension, not empirical inference.

## Limitations

The workflow uses simplified mechanisms, simulated parameters, no real participant data, no causal identification strategy, and no validated peer-relations, school-climate, friendship-quality, bullying, digital-comparison, or self-concept scale.

## Extension paths

The structure can be extended with validated survey measures, classroom observations, peer-network data, school connectedness instruments, bullying and restorative-practice records, digital peer-world measures, mixed-method interviews, and stronger causal designs.
