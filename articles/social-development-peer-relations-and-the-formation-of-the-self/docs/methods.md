# Methods

## Overview

This workflow creates a synthetic longitudinal panel for social-development research. It demonstrates how peer relations, belonging, exclusion, school climate, digital comparison, and the formation of the social self can be represented across repeated waves and nested school contexts.

## Design

The synthetic panel includes:

- child/adolescent baseline characteristics;
- repeated observation waves;
- school connectedness, teacher support, inclusion climate, anti-bullying climate, and restorative practice access;
- time-varying peer support, friendship quality, family support, social interpretation, exclusion, bullying exposure, and digital comparison;
- social support context;
- social-self score;
- social-development profiles.

## Modeling strategy

The repository uses synthetic data generation, dynamic regression, multilevel modeling, scenario comparison, SQL summaries, and compiled-language validation utilities.

## Interpretation

The models illustrate social development, peer relations, social-self formation, belonging, exclusion, institutional climate, digital comparison, and protective context. They are scaffolds for reasoning, teaching, and methodological extension.
