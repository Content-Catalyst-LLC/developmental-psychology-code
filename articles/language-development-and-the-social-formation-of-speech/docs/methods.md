# Methods

## Overview

This workflow creates a synthetic longitudinal panel for language development, social interaction, and communication ecology research. It demonstrates how language growth can be represented across repeated waves and nested language contexts.

## Design

The synthetic panel includes:

- child-level baseline language characteristics;
- repeated observation waves;
- context-level language ecology variables;
- time-varying responsive interaction, shared reading, joint attention, turn-taking, and stress;
- hearing support and multilingual exposure;
- home-language recognition, book access, and early education quality;
- composite language support context;
- broad language-development outcome;
- language-development profiles.

## Modeling strategy

The repository uses synthetic data generation, dynamic regression, nonlinear growth terms, multilevel modeling, scenario comparison, SQL summaries, and compiled-language validation utilities.

## Interpretation

The models illustrate language development as a socially formed, embodied, multilingual, institutionally shaped process. They are not intended to replace validated language assessment, but to support reproducible reasoning about mechanisms, supports, stressors, and developmental variation.
