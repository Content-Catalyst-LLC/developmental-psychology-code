# Research Notes

## Article
Language Development and the Social Formation of Speech

## Research frame

This repository operationalizes language development as a longitudinal developmental process shaped by responsive interaction, shared reading, joint attention, conversational turn-taking, hearing support, multilingual exposure, home-language recognition, book access, early education quality, language ecology, chronic stress, and institutional recognition of communication differences.

## Core constructs

- language score;
- baseline language;
- responsive interaction;
- shared reading;
- joint attention;
- conversational turn-taking;
- hearing support;
- multilingual exposure;
- language ecology support;
- book access;
- early education quality;
- home-language recognition;
- chronic stress;
- current stress;
- language support context;
- language-development profile.

## Research-grade elements

- synthetic longitudinal child language-development panel data;
- context-level language ecology metadata;
- nonlinear and state-dependent language-growth modeling;
- multilevel context modeling;
- scenario comparison for language support, reading access, hearing support, multilingual recognition, stress reduction, and combined support;
- SQL schema and analytical queries;
- Python and R workflows;
- compiled-language validation and summary utilities;
- reproducibility, validation, responsible-use, and model-card documentation.
