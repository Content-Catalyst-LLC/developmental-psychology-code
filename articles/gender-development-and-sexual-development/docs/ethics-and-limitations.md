# Ethics and Limitations

## Not a clinical, school-discipline, identity-screening, or risk-prediction tool

This repository must not be used for diagnosis, gender identity screening, sexual orientation inference, clinical triage, school discipline, student tracking, surveillance, outing, placement decisions, mental-health prediction, risk scoring, or any high-stakes decision.

## Key risks

- reducing young people to identity, sexuality, or adjustment scores;
- conflating gender development, sexual development, sexual orientation, puberty, and sex development;
- treating identity or variation as pathology;
- treating sexuality education as behavior prediction rather than protective knowledge;
- ignoring family support, school climate, stigma, religion, culture, disability, race, class, geography, digital life, and care access;
- mistaking synthetic output for empirical evidence;
- ranking adolescents, schools, families, communities, or identities;
- using development language for surveillance, discipline, coercion, or shame.

## Responsible use

Use this repository to teach and explore:

- developmental distinctions among gender, sex development, sexual development, and sexual orientation;
- puberty as embodied developmental timing;
- support, recognition, consent knowledge, school connectedness, and health education;
- stigma, exclusion, harassment, misinformation, and coercion as contextual risks;
- synthetic longitudinal modeling.

Responsible real-world work requires validated measures, developmental expertise, privacy protection, consent and assent safeguards, anti-bias procedures, disability rights awareness, trauma-informed practice, cultural humility, and respect for dignity and safety.
