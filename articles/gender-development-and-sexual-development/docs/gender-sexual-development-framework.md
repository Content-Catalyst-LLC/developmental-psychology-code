# Gender Development and Sexual Development Framework

## Core constructs

| Construct | Developmental meaning |
|---|---|
| Pubertal progression | Embodied developmental timing and sexual maturation |
| Family support | Relational safety, communication, acceptance, dignity, and guidance |
| Social recognition | Whether identity, expression, and embodied development are respected |
| Consent knowledge | Understanding of boundaries, bodily autonomy, communication, and sexual health |
| School connectedness | Belonging, supportive adults, peer safety, and institutional inclusion |
| School climate | Broader institutional safety and respect |
| Health education quality | Accuracy and developmental appropriateness of sexual-health education |
| Anti-harassment support | Institutional capacity to prevent and respond to harassment |
| Current stigma | Time-varying stigma, exclusion, shame, harassment, misinformation, or coercive pressure |
| Chronic stigma | Persistent contextual risk exposure |
| Protective context | Composite support, recognition, knowledge, connectedness, and institutional protection |
| Adjustment score | Synthetic adolescent developmental adjustment outcome |

## Interpretation

This workflow does not infer real gender identity, sexual orientation, sexual behavior, or clinical status. It models how support, knowledge, recognition, stigma, and institutional climate can shape broad adolescent adjustment in synthetic data.
