# Research Notes

## Article

**Gender Development and Sexual Development**

## Purpose of this code folder

This folder translates the article's developmental argument into a reproducible, multi-language research scaffold. It treats gender development and sexual development as related but distinct developmental processes shaped by embodiment, puberty, family support, social recognition, consent knowledge, school connectedness, health education, anti-harassment support, stigma, digital/peer context, and access to care.

## Core research commitments

1. **Distinction matters**  
   Gender development, sex development, sexual development, sexual orientation, and adolescent adjustment should not be collapsed into a single process.

2. **Development is embodied and social**  
   Puberty and bodily development matter, but bodies are interpreted through family, peer, school, cultural, institutional, medical, and digital contexts.

3. **Knowledge is protective**  
   Age-appropriate knowledge about bodies, puberty, consent, boundaries, and sexual health supports safety and development.

4. **Stigma is contextual risk**  
   Developmental risk often comes from stigma, harassment, misinformation, coercion, exclusion, and lack of support rather than from identity itself.

5. **Protective context matters**  
   Family support, recognition, school connectedness, health education, and anti-harassment support can buffer contextual risk.

6. **Ethical caution is part of the method**  
   These scripts are synthetic demonstrations, not clinical, educational-placement, identity-screening, discipline, surveillance, or risk-prediction tools.

## What makes the folder research-grade

- synthetic longitudinal adolescent data with repeated waves;
- adolescent-level and school-level variables;
- pubertal progression, family support, social recognition, consent knowledge, school connectedness, stigma, chronic stigma, school climate, health education quality, anti-harassment support, protective context, and adjustment;
- dynamic modeling with lagged outcomes;
- support/stigma/protective-context interaction modeling;
- scenario analysis for support, education, connectedness, anti-harassment, stigma reduction, and combined protective context;
- SQL schema and analytical queries;
- Python and R workflows;
- compiled-language validation and summary utilities;
- explicit ethics and reproducibility notes;
- notes in every subdirectory.
