# Research Notes

## Article
Puberty, Embodiment, and Adolescent Transition

## Research frame

Puberty is modeled as biological maturation lived through embodiment, peer comparison, privacy, family response, school support, health education, disability accommodation, digital visibility, stigma, and inequality.

## Research-grade elements

- synthetic longitudinal adolescent panel data;
- adolescent-level, school-level, and time-varying variables;
- dynamic modeling with lagged adjustment score;
- stigma-risk and pubertal-timing trajectory comparisons;
- scenario analysis for family support, school support, health education, privacy protection, menstrual support, disability accommodation, anti-harassment climate, digital safety, stigma reduction, and combined protective context;
- Python, R, SQL, C, C++, Go, Rust, Julia, and Fortran examples;
- explicit ethics, limitations, and reproducibility notes.
