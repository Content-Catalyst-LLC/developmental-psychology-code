# Research Notes: outputs

This subfolder is part of the research-grade companion directory for:

**Developmental Systems Theory and the Ecology of Human Growth**

## Function of this folder

The `outputs/` folder contributes to a reproducible, multi-language research workflow for modeling nested, reciprocal, ecological, and relational development: biological sensitivity, family support, peer belonging, school climate, neighborhood safety, service access, material security, ecological stress, intervention exposure, and developmental outcomes over time.

## Article directory

`articles/developmental-systems-theory-and-the-ecology-of-human-growth`

## Research commitments

- development as person-context coaction;
- ecology as part of development rather than background;
- biology as embodied and contextual;
- proximal processes, nested systems, and path dependence;
- support and stress as system-level variables;
- ethical caution against high-stakes prediction or deficit labeling;
- transparent notes and schemas.
