# Methods

## Overview

This workflow models developmental systems theory as a longitudinal, nested ecological process. The synthetic data include children observed repeatedly across time and nested within schools and neighborhoods.

The model includes:

- biological sensitivity;
- family support;
- peer belonging;
- school climate;
- curriculum opportunity;
- neighborhood safety;
- service access;
- material security;
- ecological support;
- ecological stress;
- intervention exposure;
- development score;
- ecological-support profile.

## Conceptual interpretation

### Biological sensitivity

Biological sensitivity represents person-level responsiveness or susceptibility. It is not treated as fixed destiny.

### Relational experience

Family support and peer belonging represent recurring proximal processes through which development is organized.

### Ecological context

School climate, curriculum opportunity, neighborhood safety, service access, and material security represent nested contexts that structure opportunity, stress, learning, and support.

### Ecological stress

Ecological stress is modeled as a time-varying burden shaped partly by family, school, neighborhood, and material conditions.

### Intervention exposure

Intervention exposure represents additional support. It is modeled as a system-level opportunity rather than a simple individual treatment effect.

## Synthetic-data caution

The data are not empirical findings. They are simulated for reproducible demonstration only.
