# Developmental Systems Framework

## Developmental systems variables

| Domain | Developmental relevance |
|---|---|
| Biological sensitivity | Embodied responsiveness, susceptibility, stress reactivity, person characteristics |
| Family support | Co-regulation, caregiving, routine, safety, attachment, advocacy |
| Peer belonging | Social recognition, status, identity, participation, friendship |
| School climate | Safety, expectations, discipline, support, institutional trust |
| Curriculum opportunity | Cognitive development, skill, aspiration, symbolic access |
| Neighborhood safety | Mobility, stress, play, public life, threat exposure |
| Service access | Prevention, health care, disability support, family support |
| Material security | Housing, food, transportation, stability, energy, time |
| Ecological stress | Accumulated contextual burden across systems |
| Intervention exposure | Additional system support that may redirect trajectory |

## Interpretation

This workflow does not ask whether nature or nurture wins. It asks how developmental outcomes change when person characteristics, relationships, institutions, material conditions, timing, support, and stress coact over time.
