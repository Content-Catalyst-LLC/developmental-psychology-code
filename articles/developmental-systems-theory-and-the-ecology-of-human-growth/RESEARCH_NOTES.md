# Research Notes

## Article

**Developmental Systems Theory and the Ecology of Human Growth**

## Purpose of this code folder

This folder translates the article's developmental argument into a reproducible, multi-language research scaffold. It treats development as nested, reciprocal, embodied, ecological, and historical rather than as the output of one isolated variable.

## Core research commitments

1. **Development is relational**  
   The developing person and the developmental context are modeled as mutually organizing, not as separable causes.

2. **Ecology is part of development**  
   Family, peer, school, neighborhood, service access, material security, and institutional supports are modeled as developmental conditions.

3. **Biology is embodied in context**  
   Biological sensitivity is represented as part of person-context coaction rather than fixed destiny.

4. **Time and path dependence matter**  
   Prior developmental state shapes later development, relationships, and ecological fit.

5. **Systems thinking requires precision**  
   The workflow names levels, mechanisms, variables, and limits rather than merely saying that everything is connected.

6. **Ethical caution is part of the method**  
   These scripts are synthetic demonstrations, not diagnostic, educational, clinical, or policy prediction tools.

## What makes the folder research-grade

- synthetic panel data with repeated developmental waves;
- child-level, school-level, neighborhood-level, and time-varying variables;
- biological sensitivity, family support, peer belonging, school climate, curriculum opportunity, neighborhood safety, service access, material security, ecological stress, and intervention exposure;
- dynamic developmental-outcome modeling;
- ecological-support profile summaries;
- scenario analysis for family, peer, school, neighborhood, service, material, and stress-reduction supports;
- SQL schema and analytical queries;
- Python and R workflows;
- compiled-language validation and summary utilities;
- explicit ethics and reproducibility notes;
- notes in every subdirectory.
