# Research Notes

## Article
The History of Developmental Psychology: From Child Study to Lifespan Science

## Research frame

This folder operationalizes the article's central historical claim: developmental psychology evolved from child study and early measurement toward a broader lifespan, ecological, culturally aware, and systems-oriented science. The field's history is modeled as a dynamic system shaped by paradigms, methods, institutions, social relevance, critique, and ethical expansion.

## Research-grade elements

- synthetic historical time series from 1900 to 2025;
- paradigm shares for child study, maturational theory, psychoanalysis, behaviorism, cognitive developmental theory, sociocultural approaches, attachment/social development, ecological systems, lifespan psychology, developmental psychopathology, neuroscience/genetics, and developmental systems;
- institutional, methodological, social-relevance, and critique indexes;
- child-centered, lifespan, ecological, systems, and broadening indexes;
- Python and R workflows;
- SQL schema and analytical queries;
- compiled-language validation and summary utilities;
- explicit methods, ethics, limitations, and reproducibility notes;
- notes in every subdirectory.
