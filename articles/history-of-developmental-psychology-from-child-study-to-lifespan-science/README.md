# The History of Developmental Psychology: From Child Study to Lifespan Science

Research-grade companion directory for the article **The History of Developmental Psychology: From Child Study to Lifespan Science**.

GitHub folder:  
https://github.com/Content-Catalyst-LLC/developmental-psychology-code/tree/main/articles/history-of-developmental-psychology-from-child-study-to-lifespan-science

## Purpose

This folder models the history of developmental psychology as a changing intellectual, institutional, methodological, and ethical field. It treats the movement from child study to lifespan science as more than a timeline of theorists. It is a broadening of developmental explanation across child study, maturational theory, psychoanalysis, behaviorism, cognitive developmental theory, sociocultural theory, attachment, ecological systems, lifespan psychology, developmental psychopathology, neuroscience, genetics, and developmental systems thinking.

## Research questions

- How can paradigm prominence be represented over historical time?
- How did developmental psychology move from child-centered observation toward lifespan, ecological, and systems-oriented science?
- How do journals, societies, funding agencies, public policy, critique, and methods shape intellectual change?
- How can the field's history be modeled without treating theory as detached from power, measurement, classification, culture, disability, and inequality?

## Ethical boundary

This is a synthetic historical-modeling scaffold. It is not a bibliometric claim unless replaced with real publication/citation data. It must not be used to rank scholars, institutions, communities, populations, children, families, disabled people, neurodivergent people, or cultural groups.
