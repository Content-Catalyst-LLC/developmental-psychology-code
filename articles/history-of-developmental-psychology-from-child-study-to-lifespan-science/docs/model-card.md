# Model Card

## Name

Synthetic developmental psychology historical paradigm model.

## Intended use

Research education, article companion materials, reproducible historical modeling, teaching paradigm shifts, and scaffolding future bibliometric work.

## Not intended for

Ranking scholars, institutions, societies, journals, populations, children, families, disabled communities, neurodivergent communities, cultural groups, or national research traditions.

## Inputs

Synthetic yearly values for paradigm prominence, institutional support, methodological advantage, social relevance, and critique.

## Outputs

Synthetic paradigm trajectories, broadening indexes, lifespan orientation indexes, institutional-methodological summaries, and scenario comparisons.

## Limitations

- no empirical bibliometric validation;
- no real publication or citation data;
- simplified historical categories;
- possible underrepresentation of non-Western, Indigenous, disability, feminist, Black, decolonial, and community-based developmental traditions;
- numerical modeling cannot replace careful historical interpretation.
