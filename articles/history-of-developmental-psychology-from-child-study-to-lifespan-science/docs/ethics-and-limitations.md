# Ethics and Limitations

## Not a ranking tool

This repository must not be used to rank individual scholars, universities, societies, journals, populations, cultures, children, families, disabled people, neurodivergent people, racialized groups, or national research traditions.

## Synthetic-data limitation

The historical data are simulated. They are useful for conceptual modeling and teaching, but they do not prove empirical claims about publication counts or paradigm dominance.

## Key risks

- treating simulated paradigm shares as real bibliometric evidence;
- simplifying intellectual history into a single numerical trend;
- erasing marginalized scholars, communities, cultural traditions, and non-Western developmental knowledge;
- treating Western developmental psychology as the universal history of developmental thought;
- ignoring the field's role in measurement, classification, exclusion, eugenics, disability stigma, racial hierarchy, and educational sorting;
- celebrating progress without acknowledging harm.

## Responsible use

Use this repository to teach how developmental psychology broadened and how research fields change. For empirical work, document data provenance, sampling rules, coding decisions, uncertainty, and archival limitations.
