# Ethics and Limitations

Developmental research methods affect how researchers represent children, adolescents, adults, families, schools, communities, disabled people, older adults, and historically situated cohorts.

## Key risks

- Treating cross-sectional differences as if they prove within-person change.
- Treating cohort differences as if they are universal developmental laws.
- Ignoring attrition and missing data.
- Overgeneralizing from narrow samples.
- Treating statistical trajectories as deterministic life paths.
- Failing to account for context, inequality, culture, and historical period.

## Responsible use

Responsible developmental methods should:

- match claims to the design;
- distinguish age, period, and cohort whenever possible;
- document missingness and attrition;
- use validated measures when making empirical claims;
- protect participant privacy;
- avoid stigmatizing groups;
- communicate uncertainty clearly;
- include context when interpreting developmental pathways.
