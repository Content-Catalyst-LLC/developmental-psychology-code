# Methods

## Overview
This workflow creates a synthetic longitudinal panel for moral-development research. It demonstrates how conscience, moral support context, and moral action can be represented across repeated waves and nested school or community contexts.

## Design
The synthetic panel includes child/adolescent baseline characteristics, repeated observation waves, school moral climate, restorative practice access, punitive inconsistency, anti-bullying climate, digital moral safety, time-varying caregiving guidance, empathy, peer fairness, harm recognition, repair opportunity, exclusion, peer pressure, conscience score, and moral action score.

## Modeling strategy
The repository uses synthetic data generation, dynamic regression, multilevel modeling, scenario comparison, SQL summaries, and compiled-language validation utilities.

## Interpretation
The models illustrate conscience formation, empathy, fairness, repair, self-regulation, institutional consistency, peer pressure, exclusion, and moral action. They are scaffolds for reasoning, teaching, and methodological extension.
