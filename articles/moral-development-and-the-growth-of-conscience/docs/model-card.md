# Model Card

## Name
Synthetic moral development and conscience trajectory model.

## Intended use
Research education, article companion materials, methods teaching, longitudinal analysis practice, moral-development modeling, and reproducible workflow development.

## Inputs
Synthetic variables representing baseline morality, caregiving guidance, empathic sensitivity, peer fairness, self-regulation, harm recognition, restorative practice access, punitive inconsistency, anti-bullying climate, digital moral safety, exclusion, peer pressure, conscience score, and moral action score.

## Outputs
Longitudinal synthetic panel, moral profile summaries, school/context summaries, dynamic regression outputs, trajectory plots, scenario comparisons, and validation summaries.

## Data notes
All generated data are synthetic and parameterized for transparency. The design is suitable for demonstration and extension, not empirical inference.

## Limitations
The workflow uses simplified mechanisms, simulated parameters, no real participant data, no causal identification strategy, and no validated moral-judgment, empathy, conscience, disciplinary-climate, or school-climate measurement scale.

## Extension paths
The structure can be extended with validated measures, classroom observations, peer-network data, disciplinary records, restorative-practice documentation, mixed-method interviews, digital-behavior datasets, and stronger causal designs.
