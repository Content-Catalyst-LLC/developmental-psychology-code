# Moral Development Framework

## Core developmental claims
1. Moral development is more than obedience or rule knowledge.
2. Conscience grows through emotion, relationship, repair, and repeated social experience.
3. Empathy, guilt, fairness, and self-regulation develop in relation to context.
4. Peer worlds are moral-development settings where fairness, loyalty, exclusion, and repair are practiced.
5. School and institutional climates teach morality through enacted fairness or inconsistency.
6. Restorative repair and punitive inconsistency have different developmental meanings.
7. Digital environments extend moral development into visibility, cruelty, bystander behavior, and accountability.
8. Moral action under pressure depends on conscience, regulation, group norms, peer pressure, and social risk.

## Analytical structure

```text
M_it = rho * M_i,t-1 + beta * time + gamma * context_it - lambda * exclusion_it + error_it
```

Where `M_it` is conscience score, `M_i,t-1` is prior conscience, `context_it` is moral support context, and `exclusion_it` is current exclusion or destabilizing moral stress.

```text
B_it = eta_1 * conscience_it + eta_2 * self_regulation_it - eta_3 * peer_pressure_it + eta_4 * moral_context_it + error_it
```

This distinction matters because moral understanding and moral action can diverge under pressure.
