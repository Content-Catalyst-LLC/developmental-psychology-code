# Methods

## Overview

This workflow models education and schooling as a longitudinal developmental system. The synthetic data include students nested within schools and observed repeatedly across time.

The model includes:

- teacher support;
- peer belonging;
- school stress;
- family support;
- academic confidence;
- school climate;
- curriculum opportunity;
- restorative practice;
- resource capacity;
- intervention exposure;
- connectedness score;
- development score;
- school-support profile.

## Conceptual interpretation

### Teacher support

Teacher support represents relational recognition, guidance, classroom care, instructional support, and the student's experience of being seen.

### Peer belonging

Peer belonging represents social inclusion, friendship, safety, recognition, and the lived peer climate of schooling.

### School climate

School climate represents the institutional atmosphere in which learning, discipline, safety, and support are organized.

### Curriculum opportunity

Curriculum opportunity represents the richness, depth, and developmental availability of academic learning.

### School stress

School stress represents academic pressure, social threat, disciplinary strain, chronic mismatch, or institutional burden.

### Connectedness

Connectedness is modeled as a mediator-like developmental condition linking school relationships, climate, and student development.

## Synthetic-data caution

The data are not empirical findings. They are simulated for reproducible demonstration only.
