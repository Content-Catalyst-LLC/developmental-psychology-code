# Research Notes

## Article

**Education, Schooling, and Developmental Formation**

## Purpose of this code folder

This folder translates the article's developmental argument into a reproducible, multi-language research scaffold. It treats schooling as a developmental institution shaped by relationships, connectedness, peer worlds, curriculum opportunity, school climate, health, discipline, family support, and institutional resources.

## Core research commitments

1. **Schooling is developmental formation**  
   Schools do not simply measure development. They organize repeated experiences through which students form habits, confidence, belonging, self-regulation, academic identity, and future orientation.

2. **Connectedness is developmental infrastructure**  
   Teacher support, peer belonging, and school climate are modeled as developmental variables, not soft add-ons.

3. **Opportunity is institutional**  
   Curriculum opportunity, resource capacity, restorative practice, and school climate are school-level conditions that shape student trajectories.

4. **Stress and exclusion matter**  
   School stress, discipline climate, and belonging influence whether school becomes developmental support or developmental burden.

5. **Ethical caution is part of the method**  
   These scripts are synthetic demonstrations, not student prediction systems, accountability metrics, or high-stakes decision tools.

## What makes the folder research-grade

- synthetic panel data with repeated developmental waves;
- student-level, school-level, and time-varying variables;
- connectedness, development, curriculum opportunity, support, stress, intervention exposure, and school climate;
- dynamic developmental-outcome modeling;
- school-support profile summaries;
- scenario analysis for connectedness, restorative practice, and curriculum opportunity;
- SQL schema and analytical queries;
- Python and R workflows;
- compiled-language validation and summary utilities;
- explicit ethics and reproducibility notes;
- notes in every subdirectory.
