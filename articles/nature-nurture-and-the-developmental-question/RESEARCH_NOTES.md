# Research Notes

## Article

**Nature, Nurture, and the Developmental Question**

## Purpose of this code folder

This folder translates the article's developmental argument into a reproducible, multi-language research scaffold. It treats nature and nurture as interacting developmental systems rather than separable causal blocks. The workflow models biological sensitivity, structural risk, chronic adversity, caregiver support, acute stress, institutional support, disability support, resource stability, intervention exposure, protective context, and developmental outcomes over time.

## Core research commitments

1. **Reject the false binary**  
   Development is not produced by nature or nurture alone, but by interaction, timing, feedback, and context.

2. **Model differential susceptibility**  
   Biological sensitivity can amplify response to both supportive and harmful environments.

3. **Treat nurture as institutional and unequal**  
   Nurture includes families, schools, clinics, neighborhoods, public policy, disability supports, material resources, environmental exposure, and cultural context.

4. **Represent path dependence**  
   Earlier developmental status shapes later developmental status through feedback loops.

5. **Keep biology developmental**  
   Biological influence is real, but not destiny.

6. **Use developmental explanation ethically**  
   These scripts are synthetic demonstrations, not genetic screening, child ranking, diagnosis, school placement, disability classification, or high-stakes prediction tools.

## What makes the folder research-grade

- synthetic longitudinal data with repeated waves;
- child-level and school/context-level variables;
- biological sensitivity, caregiver support, acute stress, structural risk, chronic adversity, institutional support, disability support, resource stability, intervention exposure, protective context, and developmental outcome;
- dynamic modeling with lagged outcomes;
- gene-environment and biological-sensitivity-by-context interactions;
- structural-risk and protective-context trajectory comparisons;
- scenario analysis for supports, stress reduction, intervention, and combined protective context;
- SQL schema and analytical queries;
- Python and R workflows;
- compiled-language validation and summary utilities;
- explicit ethics and reproducibility notes;
- notes in every subdirectory.
