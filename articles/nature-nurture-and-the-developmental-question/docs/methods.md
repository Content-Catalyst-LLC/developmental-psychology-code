# Methods

## Overview

This workflow models development as a transactional relation among biological sensitivity, structural risk, caregiver support, acute stress, institutional support, disability support, resource stability, intervention exposure, and protective context.

The synthetic data include:

- repeated developmental waves;
- child-level biological sensitivity;
- child-level structural risk and chronic adversity;
- time-varying caregiver support and acute stress;
- school/context-level institutional support, disability support, and resource stability;
- later intervention exposure;
- protective context;
- developmental outcome;
- dynamic path dependence.

## Conceptual interpretation

### Biological sensitivity

Biological sensitivity represents a latent sensitivity or reactivity parameter. It is not a real genotype, biomarker, diagnosis, or trait.

### Gene-environment interaction

The effect of support or stress can vary with biological sensitivity. This represents differential susceptibility.

### Structural risk

Structural risk represents conditions such as poverty, instability, exclusion, discrimination, environmental exposure, family stress, or policy neglect.

### Protective context

Protective context aggregates caregiver support, institutional support, disability support, resource stability, and intervention exposure.

### Path dependence

Lagged developmental score represents the idea that earlier developmental states shape later states.

## Synthetic-data caution

The data are not empirical findings. They are simulated for reproducible demonstration only.
