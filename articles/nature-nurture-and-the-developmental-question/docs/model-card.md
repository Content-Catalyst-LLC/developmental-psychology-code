# Model Card

## Name

Synthetic nature-nurture, gene-environment interaction, and developmental context model.

## Intended use

Research education, reproducible-method scaffolding, differential-susceptibility demonstration, structural-risk modeling, protective-context scenario analysis, and conceptual modeling.

## Not intended for

Genetic screening, clinical diagnosis, disability classification, school placement, student tracking, child ranking, family-risk scoring, eligibility decisions, surveillance, or any high-stakes decision-making.

## Inputs

Synthetic variables representing biological sensitivity, baseline functioning, structural risk, chronic adversity, family support context, caregiver support, acute stress, institutional support, disability support, resource stability, intervention exposure, protective context, and developmental outcome.

## Outputs

Synthetic developmental trajectories, structural-risk summaries, protective-context summaries, sensitivity profiles, school/context summaries, and scenario comparisons.

## Limitations

- No empirical validation.
- No causal evidence.
- No person-level predictive validity.
- No actual genetics, biomarkers, diagnosis, or clinical measures.
- Synthetic parameter values.
- Simplified school and developmental structure.
- Potential misuse if interpreted as real child analytics.

## Ethical position

The model treats children and families as developing within social systems, not as targets for ranking or surveillance. It should never be used to classify or predict real children, families, schools, disabled people, neurodivergent people, or marginalized communities.
